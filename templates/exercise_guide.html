💪 Exercise & Physical Activity Guide

Personalized workout recommendations for diabetes prevention and management

🚶 Walking

Intensity: Moderate
Duration: 30-60 minutes
Frequency: 5 days/week
Benefits
  • Improves insulin sensitivity
  • Lowers blood sugar naturally
  • Strengthens heart and bones
  • Easy for beginners

🚴 Cycling

Intensity: Moderate to High
Duration: 30-45 minutes
Frequency: 3-4 days/week
Benefits
  • Burns significant calories
  • Low impact on joints
  • Increases cardiovascular fitness
  • Great for weight management

🏊 Swimming

Intensity: Moderate to High
Duration: 30-45 minutes
Frequency: 3-4 days/week
Benefits
  • Full-body workout
  • Excellent for joint health
  • Improves lung capacity
  • Low injury risk

🧘 Yoga

Intensity: Low to Moderate
Duration: 30-60 minutes
Frequency: 3-5 days/week
Benefits
  • Reduces stress and cortisol
  • Improves flexibility
  • Enhances mind-body connection
  • Helps blood sugar control

💪 Strength Training

Intensity: High
Duration: 30-45 minutes
Frequency: 2-3 days/week
Benefits
  • Builds muscle mass
  • Increases metabolism
  • Improves glucose uptake
  • Boosts overall strength

🕺 Dancing

Intensity: Moderate
Duration: 30-60 minutes
Frequency: 3-4 days/week
Benefits
  • Fun and engaging
  • Improves coordination
  • Great for cardiovascular health
  • Social and mentally uplifting

⚠️ Important Exercise Guidelines

  • Start Gradually: Begin with low intensity and gradually increase
  • Warm-up & Cool-down: Always spend 5-10 minutes warming up and cooling down
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink water before, during, and after exercise
  • Monitor Blood Sugar: Check levels before and after intense exercise
  • Wear Proper Shoes: Invest in good quality athletic footwear
  • Consistency: Regular exercise is better than sporadic intense workouts
  • Listen to Your Body: Stop if you experience pain or extreme fatigue
  • Consult Your Doctor: Get medical clearance before starting new exercise programs
  • Rest Days: Include at least 1-2 rest days per week for recovery
  • Mix It Up: Combine cardio, strength, and flexibility training for best results
