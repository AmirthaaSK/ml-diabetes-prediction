🍎 Diabetes-Friendly Diet Guide

Nutritional guidelines to help manage blood glucose levels

✅ Foods to Eat

Leafy Greens

Spinach, kale, lettuce. Low carb, high fiber. Rich in vitamins and minerals.

Whole Grains

Brown rice, oats, whole wheat. Lower glycemic index, slower glucose rise.

Lean Proteins

Chicken, fish, tofu, legumes. Essential for muscle maintenance and satiety.

Fatty Fish

Salmon, mackerel, sardines. Rich in omega-3s, reduces inflammation.

Vegetables

Broccoli, cauliflower, carrots. Nutrient-dense, low in calories and carbs.

Berries

Blueberries, strawberries, blackberries. High in antioxidants and fiber.

Nuts & Seeds

Almonds, walnuts, flax seeds. Healthy fats, improve cholesterol levels.

Avocados

Monounsaturated fats, potassium. Excellent for heart and metabolic health.

❌ Foods to Avoid

Sugary Drinks

Soda, juices, sweet tea. Causes rapid blood glucose spikes.

Refined Carbs

White bread, white rice, pastries. High glycemic index, poor nutritional value.

Processed Foods

Fast food, packaged snacks. High in sodium, sugar, and unhealthy fats.

Desserts & Candy

Cakes, cookies, chocolate. Direct sugar intake causes severe glucose spikes.

Full-Fat Dairy

Whole milk, high-fat cheese. High in saturated fats and calories.

Alcohol

Beer, sweet wine, cocktails. Can cause unpredictable blood sugar changes.

Fried Foods

Chips, fried chicken, donuts. High in trans fats and calories.

High-Sodium Foods

Canned soups, processed meats. Increases blood pressure risk.

📅 Sample Daily Meal Plan

Day 1: Balanced Nutrition

🌅 Breakfast (7:00 AM)
Oatmeal with berries, almonds, and unsweetened almond milk. Black coffee or green tea.
🥗 Mid-Morning Snack (10:00 AM)
Apple with 1 tablespoon almond butter
🍽️ Lunch (12:30 PM)
Grilled salmon, brown rice, steamed broccoli with olive oil
🥤 Afternoon Snack (3:00 PM)
Handful of walnuts and a cucumber
🍴 Dinner (6:30 PM)
Lean chicken breast, sweet potato, leafy green salad with vinaigrette

💡 Important Dietary Tips

  • Portion Control: Use smaller plates, measure portions carefully
  • Meal Timing: Eat at regular intervals to maintain stable blood sugar
  • Fiber Intake: Aim for 25-30g of fiber per day to slow glucose absorption
  • Hydration: Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily
  • Glycemic Index: Choose foods with low to medium glycemic index values
  • Food Labels: Read nutrition labels carefully, watch for hidden sugars
  • Cooking Methods: Prefer grilling, baking, or steaming over frying
  • Consult Professionals: Work with a registered dietitian for personalized advice
